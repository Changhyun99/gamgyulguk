Three Generations of
Jeju Citrus Farming

Gamgyulguk was born from a family legacy spanning three generations of Jeju citrus cultivation. We grow our mandarins in the island's mineral-rich volcanic soil, under the abundant Jeju sunshine and clean island breezes — with no chemical fertilizers, only natural farming methods that produce fruit with a perfect balance of sweetness and acidity.

Citrus Varieties

Nature's Full Spectrum

Each variety grown on our Jeju farm has its own character, flavor profile, and season. Discover the remarkable diversity of Jeju citrus.

Oct – Jan

Jeju Mandarin

The iconic Jeju citrus, grown in open fields. A well-balanced blend of natural sweetness and gentle acidity with thin skin and abundant juice.

Dec – Mar

Hallabong

Named after Mt. Halla, this premium mandarin features an unmistakable dome-shaped top. Exceptionally sweet, juicy, and richly aromatic — the premier gift variety.

Jan – Mar

Cheonhyehyang

Literally "heaven's fragrant gift." Its intensely sweet perfume fills a room the moment it's peeled. Silky texture with an extraordinarily high Brix sugar level.

Jan – Feb

Redhyang

Distinguished by its reddish blush flesh, this large, nearly seedless variety delivers a superb balance of sweet and tart in every bite.

Nov – Dec

Hwanggeumhyang

The "golden fragrance" citrus. Thin golden skin hides intensely sweet, densely packed fruit that is easy to peel and irresistibly aromatic.

Aug – Sep

Green Citrus (Unripe)

Harvested while still green for its vibrant acidity and intense aroma. Prized as an ingredient for beverages, syrups, and culinary sauces.
